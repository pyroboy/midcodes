## Core Values

### 1. Barkada-Centric
Everything revolves around playing WITH people. Not solo booking — group experiences.
- Group booking with shareable links
- Barkada split payments via GCash
- Auto-create group chats for booked sessions
- "Rally your barkada" — the invitation is always plural

### 2. Community First
The app is a home base for PH picklers, not just a transaction.
- Open community chat channels
- Per-venue channels where regulars hang out
- "Looking for game" boards — find strangers who become friends
- Skill-based groups so beginners aren't intimidated

### 3. Speed & Simplicity
Faster than messaging venue owners on Viber. Faster than scrolling Facebook groups.
- Book in under 30 seconds: name + time + pay → done
- One-tap join for open play sessions
- Auto-snipe: we book it for you the moment a slot opens

## Psychology Tactics

### Social Proof
- Show real-time: "42 picklers online right now"
- "8/12 spots filled" — scarcity + social validation
- Player count per venue: "127 players have played here"
- Barkada count: "15 barkadas booked this week"

### FOMO / Urgency
- "3 spots left" badges on open play sessions
- "Courts filling fast for Saturday morning"
- Auto-snipe framing: "Don't miss your regular slot — let us grab it for you"
- Time-based: "Available in the next hour" as default filter

### Belonging / Identity
- "You're a pickler" — identity reinforcement
- Skill level as badge, not barrier: "Proud Beginner", "Intermediate Grinder", "Advanced Player"
- Venue "regulars" feature — show who plays where often
- "Your home court" — personalize the experience

### Loss Aversion
- "Your Tuesday 6PM slot at BGC was taken" → "Set up Auto-Snipe so you never miss it again"
- "3 of your barkada already booked — you're the missing player"
- "This court was available 5 minutes ago" — urgency to act fast

### Reciprocity / Micro-commitments
- "Just set your preferences" (low barrier → leads to booking)
- "Join the community" (free → eventually books courts)
- Share a game → invite friends → they join too

## Voice & Tone

### Personality
- **Friendly** — like a kumpare who plays pickle
- **Energetic** — "Tara, laro tayo!"
- **Inclusive** — beginners welcome, no elitism
- **Local** — Taglish, Bisaya-friendly, Filipino humor
- **Not corporate** — no "leverage synergies" talk

### Language Examples

**Do say:**
- "Laro tayo!" (Let's play!)
- "Rally your barkada"
- "Your next game is waiting"
- "Courts near you — right now"
- "GCash na, book na" (Pay now, book now)

**Don't say:**
- "Optimize your court utilization"
- "Schedule a facility reservation"
- "Premium booking experience"
- Anything that sounds like enterprise SaaS

### Pickleball Terms to Use Naturally
- Dink, kitchen, rally, paddle, pickler
- Open play, rec play, drill session
- Barkada booking, group play
- "See you on the court!"

## Visual Identity

### Colors
- **Primary:** Green (sports energy, "go!", court surface)
- **Accent:** Warm yellow/amber (friendly, Filipino warmth)
- **Background:** Clean white/light gray

### Typography
- **Headings:** Bold, tight tracking, high-energy

- "Ta" as the icon mark — short, bold, recognizable
- Incorporate a paddle or ball subtly

- Colors: green primary
